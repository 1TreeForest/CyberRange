About Telecaster Guitars


What is a Telecaster?


Originally dubbed the Broadcaster in the 50s by Fender, the Telecaster is one of the most iconic guitar bodies of all time. Its debated to even be the first solidbody guitar ever. The Teles rich history spans decades as the axe of choice for countless guitar legends, from George Harrison and Jimmy Page to Jeff Buckley and Brad Paisley. This guitar body draws the crowd of virtuosos and tone heads from all genres for its definitive twang and incredible playability. While other vendors have drawn inspiration to create T-style guitars, the original Telecaster holds up to this day within the guitar community for good reason. Fast, rhythmic, and comfortable are words that come to mind when you think of why musicians love this marvel of an instrument so much.


What types of Telecasters are there?


Telecasters come in a plethora of vivid, musically inspiring colors, but theres no Tele more recognizable than a butterscotch blonde solidbody. Available across dozens of series with various signature looks and configurations, the Tele makes itself at home with both traditional and modern guitar players. Consider a Fender Boxer series for sleek cutaways and dual humbuckers, or a Fender American Professional II for a finish and dual single-coil pickup configuration that have withstood the test of time. No matter which one you choose, youre getting an artfully crafted Telecaster that plays as good as it looks.


Does Squier make a Telecaster?


If youre a budding guitarist learning the ropes or strings of your instrument, then Squier has the Telecaster for you at an affordable price. Classic Vibe and Affinity series Telecasters come in just as many options as its world-renowned counterpart, ensuring youll be comfortable and confident at your first gig.
