Hands in HIROSHIMA, MON AMOUR.

HIROSHIMA, MON AMOUR is in many ways our CITIZEN KANE: between Duras and Resnais the film seems to express all the possibilities of cinema magically encapsulated in just 92 minutesat once a radical formal experiment, a philosophical dissertation on memory and trauma, and an endlessly moving love story. The themes of memory and time expressed with this level of formal sophistication are in many ways still unmatched in film history It was the eye catching formal elements that first impressed us, but revisiting it during the years it is the psychologically complex love story that has made the most lasting impression.Joachim Trier and Eskil Vogt
