A Singaporean in China

January 29th, 2022 | Author: Contributions

I am a Singaporean based in China and yet to return home since Dec-19. Honestly speaking, China is the best place to be for the unvaccinated due to their zero tolerance for covid19 and non discriminating policy. Till today, I am not vaccinated (due to medical reason) and has no problem working and living in China.

I must say in this aspect, China is doing a good job. They respect the individual choice and there is no pressure, whatsoever to take the jab. Some of the decisions taken against the unvaccinated in Western societies (including Singapore) have shaken my faith in so called democratic countries.

If China (a communist society) can respect an individual decision and still manage to function economically well, I don’t see why a country with a population of 5 millions need to go to the extreme to impose harsh rules on unvaccinated individual. Can anyone please enlightened me?

I am planning to take my vaccines after CNY holiday (as my medical condition is now stabilizing). I have the freedom to take the vaccines when I am ready and this should be the case for everyone in Singapore. Just my thoughts.

 

Shaun
